NautLoop is a hosted platform for running fleets of AI agents in **isolated microVMs**, coordinated through a shared mesh, optionally backed by persistent memory. You state an intent — a coding task, a research question, content to produce — and a **swarm of specialist agents assembles on demand**, works in parallel across separate sandboxes, hands off results through signed peer-to-peer messages, and delivers a synthesized answer.

You bring your own Anthropic / OpenAI subscription; NautLoop supplies the infrastructure, the orchestration, and the audit trail.

## Why it exists

Running AI agents seriously today forces a choice between three bad options:

- **Run locally** — a CHF 5,000+ workstation, fan noise, heat, and you can barely run five agents in parallel.
- **Rent metered cloud** — per-vCPU-second pricing; eight always-on sandboxes ≈ $3,200/mo before token spend, and no way to use the subscription you already pay for.
- **Single-agent products** — one agent at a time, no isolation, no coordination, no memory across sessions.

NautLoop solves all three at once: flat monthly pricing, real per-agent isolation, mesh-coordinated parallelism, optional per-tenant memory, and BYO subscription so tokens cost nothing above what you already pay.
