NautLoop is in **early access** — the platform described in these docs is the one we run internally; the managed tier is being rolled out to external users in small batches.

## How to join

Email **[join@nautlense.com](mailto:join@nautlense.com?subject=NautLoop%20early%20access)** with one or two sentences about what you'd run on it (a coding fleet? research swarms? a compliance workflow?). We onboard in small waves and prioritize use cases we can support well. No credit card, no commitment — early access is free while it lasts.

## What early access includes

- The **web dashboard** — fleet view, live agent status, artifacts, session recordings
- The **`nautloop` CLI** — warm-up, run, artifacts pull, stop
- **BYO subscription flow** — your Max plan / API keys, synced into your own sandboxes
- **Optional Engram memory** for your tenant
- A direct line to the builders — your feedback lands in the backlog, not a black hole

## What ships next

- Mobile PWA with push notifications and voice input
- Preset **verb library** — "build me / research / translate / analyze" one-tap templates
- Swarm topology visualization in the Visual Loop Designer
- xNaut ⇄ NautLoop native integration (drive swarms from your local terminal)

## On the horizon

Native mobile apps, self-hosted enterprise deployment, a community verb-template marketplace, and multi-region infrastructure (currently EU-only).

## The honest part

Early access means early: no SLA yet, launch pricing may shift, and occasionally a rough edge. In exchange you get the platform at its most malleable — the people who join now decide what it becomes.
