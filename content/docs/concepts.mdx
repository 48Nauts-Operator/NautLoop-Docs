title: Key concepts
description: Sandbox, swarm, mesh, memory, verb — the five terms that describe everything NautLoop does.
---

## Sandbox

A Firecracker microVM. Boots in about two seconds, ships pre-configured with the agent CLIs and tools you need, and is **isolated at the kernel level** from every other sandbox. Dies at task end unless explicitly kept alive.

## Swarm

**One task's team** — a Lead plus its specialists, alive only for that task. The Lead counts toward the per-tier agent cap; if a decomposition wants more specialists than the cap allows, it batches (round 1, then round 2 in the same swarm) — a cap makes work *slower*, never *impossible*.

| Tier | Concurrent swarms | Agents per swarm |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | 1 | up to 5 |
| Plus | 2 | up to 10 |
| Team | 4 | up to 10 |
| Business | 8+ | up to 20 (custom above) |

## Mesh

The peer-to-peer network connecting agents. Every sandbox registers a presence on spawn and unregisters on death. Messages are **signed and logged**; agents can broadcast to the mesh, DM specific peers, or contribute to open questions.

## Memory (optional)

Engram, the per-tenant persistent knowledge store — **off unless you turn it on**. With memory enabled, every task's key findings and decisions are written back, and future tasks read from it before starting: second-time answers arrive faster and sharper. Your memory is isolated per organization, exportable as a signed archive, and deletable.

## Verb

What you ask NautLoop to do — not "spawn a sandbox" but *"build me a landing page"*, *"review this PR"*, *"investigate why revenue dropped"*. Verbs map to loop shapes; the right specialists spawn automatically.
